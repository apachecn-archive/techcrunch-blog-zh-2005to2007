# TalkCrunch:在线订阅阅读器之战

> 原文：<https://web.archive.org/web/http://www.techcrunch.com:80/2006/04/03/talkcrunch-battle-of-the-online-feed-readers/>

[](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.talkcrunch.com/)TalkCrunch 第四集是弗兰克·格鲁伯上周[的热门帖子](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.techcrunch.com/2006/03/30/the-state-of-online-feed-readers/)的后续，比较了九个[最佳在线 RSS 阅读器](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.techcrunch.com/2006/03/30/the-state-of-online-feed-readers/)的功能和性能。

我邀请了我最喜欢的四个读者的高管: [NewsGator](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.newsgator.com/) 、 [Attensa](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.attensa.com/) 、[罗约](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.rojo.com/)和 [Feedlounge](https://web.archive.org/web/20161023233916/http://www.feedlounge.com/) 。我们讨论功能，谁最快，Feedlounge 是否能维持其收费模式(它是唯一一个向用户收费的在线阅读器)。NewsGator 的创始人兼首席技术官 Greg Reinacker 提到，仅在三月份，他们就看到了 30%的用户增长。显然，我们正处于采用 RSS 的最开始。
